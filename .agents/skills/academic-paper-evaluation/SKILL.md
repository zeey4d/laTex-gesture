# Academic Paper Evaluation Criteria

When reviewing or evaluating an academic paper or a Final Year Project (FYP), you must act critically and rigorously as a professor or peer reviewer would. An "A" grade is not achieved by merely writing a lot of words, but via clear argumentation, adherence to rules, and academic rigor.

## 1. Originality and Content (25%)
- **Research Gap & Meaningful Contribution:** Did the project do something meaningful? The paper should not simply summarize existing literature. Ensure a clear research gap is addressed and a tangible contribution to the field is made, even if small.
- **Objective Alignment:** Check the introduction for the stated objectives, then immediately cross-reference the conclusion. Were the objectives actually met?

## 2. Critical Thinking and Argumentation (25%)
- **Methodological Soundness:** Is the methodology valid and robust? Ensure that the right tools and strategies were chosen to solve the stated problem.
- **Analytical Discussion:** The results must make sense and be thoroughly analyzed, not just stated. A weak paper repeats results ("Accuracy was 90%"). A strong paper analyzes them ("Accuracy was 90%, likely due to the novel filtering technique applied...").

## 3. Organization and Coherence (20%)
- **Logical Flow:** Evaluate the transitions. Does Chapter 2 logically lead into Chapter 3? A reader should effortlessly move from the introduction through to the conclusion.
- **Structural Signposting:** Headings must be helpful. One should be able to skim the table of contents and understand exactly how the argument is structured.
- **The "So What?" Question:** Ensure the paper clearly answers why the academic community or real world should care about these findings.

## 4. Quality of Literature / Research (15%)
- **Credibility and Recency:** Verify the reference list. Ensure citations correspond to recent, peer-reviewed journal articles or foundational texts. Avoid websites like Wikipedia, random blogs, or outdated papers that are no longer relevant.
- **Proper Substantiation:** Every major claim made in the paper that isn't original data must be backed by a credible citation. Opinions without proof do not belong in an academic paper.

## 5. Mechanics: Grammar, Style, and Citations (15%)
- **Formatting Guidelines:** Strict adherence to the required citation style (e.g., APA, IEEE, Harvard). Font size, margins, and the reference list must be flawless.
- **Academic Integrity:** Zero tolerance for plagiarism. Ensure rigorous paraphrasing and exact citation practices. Do not steal text without quotation marks.
- **Readability and Care:** Spelling mistakes, poor grammar, and messy formatting imply negligence. If the writing is careless, the reviewer will assume the dataset or code is careless too. Edit ruthlessly.
